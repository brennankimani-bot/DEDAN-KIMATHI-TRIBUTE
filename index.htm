DEDAN KIMATHI



FREEDOM FIGHTER



Dedan Kimathi (born Kĩmathi wa Waciũri; 31 October 1920 – 18 February 1957) was the Field Marshal and senior military leader of the Kenya Land and Freedom Army (KLFA).

The group, commonly known as the Mau Mau, fought against British colonial rule in Kenya during the 1950s.

While the British colonial government labeled him a terrorist, Kenyan nationalists revere him as a revolutionary hero and a martyr who paid the ultimate price for independence.

He was captured in October 1956 by British forces and later put on trial.

Dedan Kimathi was executed by hanging in 1957, an event that shocked many and strengthened the push for independence.

His legacy remains strong, and he is remembered as one of Kenya’s greatest heroes, contributing to the country gaining independence in 1963.



"It is better to die on our feet than to live on our knees."
